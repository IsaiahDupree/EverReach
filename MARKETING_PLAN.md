## Core Value Proposition

- **Never let a relationship go cold again.**
- EverReach is the AI-powered personal CRM that tracks relationship health (Warmth Score), tells you who needs attention, and helps you send authentic, context-rich messages in seconds.

---

## Brand Positioning

- **Category**: AI Personal CRM / Relationship Intelligence
- **For**: Professionals and teams who depend on strong networks for outcomes
- **Who need**: A reliable way to maintain hundreds of relationships without manual overhead
- **EverReach is**: An AI relationship assistant that monitors relationship health and composes personalized outreach
- **Unlike**: Traditional CRMs that track activities but don’t predict who to contact or what to say
- **We deliver**: Proactive Warmth Scores, context-aware AI messages, and a 3-minute daily routine that scales

---

## Primary Target Audience

- **Founders & Operators (SMB/Startups, 1–200 employees)**

## Secondary Target Audience

  - Prevent pipeline decay, prioritize warm leads, and personalize outreach at scale.

## Buyer Personas (ICPs)

- **ICP 1: Founder/CEO (Startup/SMB)**
  - Goals: Fundraising readiness, keep partners/investors warm, unlock network-driven deals
  - Pains: Forgetting follow-ups, scattered context, manual tracking
  - Triggers: Upcoming raise, partnership push, missed opportunity
  - Objections: “Already use Notion/Sheets/HubSpot,” “No time to set up”
  - Success: 2–3 new opportunities/mo from network; higher investor response rate

- **ICP 2: Sales Professional (AE/SDR/AM)**
  - Goals: Higher reply rates, shorter cycles, warm multi-threading
  - Pains: Pipeline cooling, blank-page syndrome, context switching
  - Triggers: New quota period, stagnant accounts, leadership focus on personalization
  - Objections: “We have Salesforce,” “AI will sound generic”
  - Success: +30–45% reply rate; +2 deals/quarter from re-warms

- **ICP 3: Consultant/Freelancer/Agency Owner**
  - Goals: Repeat business, referrals, systematic client care
  - Pains: Post-project drop-off, inconsistent outreach, lost cards
  - Triggers: Slow pipeline, missed renewals, conference season
  - Objections: “Price,” “I’ll just remember”
  - Success: 3x repeat projects; 5x referrals; 10 min/day relationship habit

## Customer Journey

- **Awareness** (Social, Search, Influencers)
  - Message: “Know exactly who to reach out to, before it’s too late.”
  - Content: 15–30s videos, carousels, founder POV posts
  - CTAs: Learn More, See How It Works
  - KPIs: CTR, video watch %, CPC, engagement rate

- **Consideration** (Website, Comparisons, Use Cases)
  - Message: Warmth Score + AI messages = fewer cold relationships
  - Content: Use-case landing pages, ROI calculators, feature demos
  - CTAs: Start Free Trial, Watch 3-min Demo
  - KPIs: LP CVR, scroll depth, demo starts

- **Trial/Activation** (Onboarding, Emails, In-App)
  - Message: Import contacts → see Warmth Scores → send 5 AI messages
  - Content: Day 0/1/3/7 sequences, checklists, in-app tours
  - CTAs: Import Now, Compose Message, Set Warmth Modes
  - KPIs: Import rate, first message time, Day-7 activation

- **Retention/Expansion** (Feature adoption)
  - Message: Routines, automation, mobile-first usage
  - Content: Tips, templates, case studies
  - CTAs: Enable automations, Try voice notes, Scan cards
  - KPIs: WAU/MAU, feature adoption, renewal rate

- **Advocacy** (Referrals, Reviews)
  - Message: “Your system works—share it.”
  - Content: Referral prompts, review asks
  - CTAs: Refer a friend, Leave a review
  - KPIs: NPS, referral rate, review volume

## Objection/Deal Killer Analysis + Handlers

- **“I already have a CRM.”** → Position as relationship layer; show integrations and warmth intelligence.
- **“No time to set it up.”** → 5-minute setup, 3-minute daily routine; offer white-glove onboarding.
- **“AI sounds generic.”** → Context-aware composer with review before send; style learning.
- **“Too expensive.”** → ROI math: 1 opportunity/year covers cost; trial first.
- **“Data privacy?”** → SOC 2, GDPR/CCPA, encryption, data residency options.

## Editorial Guidelines

- Write to one reader (“you”).
- Lead with outcomes, then features.
- Use short paragraphs and scannable bullets.
- Include proof (numbers, short quotes) early.
- Avoid jargon; define acronyms once.
- Accessibility: alt text, captions, high contrast.

---

## Brand Voice and Tone

- **Voice**: Helpful, confident, modern, human.
- **Tone**: Direct and optimistic; never hypey.
- **Do**: Specific numbers, clear benefits, real examples.
- **Don’t**: Overpromise, buzzword soup, vague claims.
